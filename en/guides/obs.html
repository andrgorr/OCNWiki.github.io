Download

For the start you will need to grab OBS or Open Broadcaster Software from their website. When installed you will see a window like this.


Scenes

In order to begin, right click the scenes box, and choose "Add Scene". Enter a name for this scene, it can be anything you want. Once the scene is created, you will need to add a source. Right click in the source box and choose "Add >". Then choose "Game capture". At this point, you will need to open Minecraft. Once in the game capture, dialogue, you will see this. Make sure to have Minecraft selected, all the other settings can be left at default. Hit OK.

Settings

Now we need to configure the video settings. Hit the "Settings" button to the right of the Sources box. I will provide multiple examples of different configurations for different types of videos. In order to achieve small file sizes, use the following settings:



Hit OK, and you're done. Make sure to note the path your video will be recorded to. Now, go to Minecraft, hit F9 (or whatever key you set to start the recording) and hit it again when you wish to stop recording. This is ideal for evidence for report and low file size recording. A 1 minute video will only have a file size of about 2.5 MB, which will also upload to Youtube in a matter of seconds.

If you wish to record higher quality videos, simply change the Max Bitrate under the "Encoding" tab to a higher value, such as 2000, 5000, or 10000. Here's a comparison video. Under the "Video" tab, turn the resolution downscale to off or a higher resolution. This will create larger video files, be warned.
